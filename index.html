    About the Artist

    Professor Franklyn Chu Egwali is a Professor of Fine Arts with emphasis on sculpture. He is a Nigerian Contemporary artist that has been practicing his craft for more than 40 years. Egwali have travelled round and have been interfacing with art lovers, art collectors, art historians and researchers interested in African contemporary masks, which he refers to as Entities. His sculptures are made of metals, fiberglass, wood, and bronze and in these materials he explores and appraises issues tehat confront his society. Egwali has the propensity and expertise to produce African traditional and contemporary art both in two and three dimensions, develop and deliver ancient/traditional African sculptures in various materials. Egwali Franklyn also conduct workshops and training on sculpture. He has written and published over fifty scholarly papers and held several solo and group art exhibitions. Egwali Franklyn holds a Bachelor of Fine Arts (BA)degree from the University of Benin, Master of Art History (MA) from Delta State University, Abraka, Master of Fine Arts (MFA) and a PhD in Visual Arts (Sculpture) from the University of Benin, Nigeria. Egwali Franklyn is married to Professor Annie Egwali and they are blessed with children.
